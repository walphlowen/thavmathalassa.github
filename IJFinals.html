Moon Jellyfish

Aurelia aurita

182 Meters Deep

The Philippines is home to the stingless jellyfish in Sohoton, Bucas Grande of Surigao de Norte. One of the harmless jellyfish species is the moon jellyfish. These species of jellyfish are unique because their stingers are so mild that they are harmless to human beings. This is why people want to experience swimming with them.

Nudibranch

Nudibranchia

700 Meters Deep

Nudibranchs exhibit a diverse range of colors and shapes, showcasing vibrant stripes, spots, and intricate appendages on their heads and backs. The distinct and vivid colors serve not only to differentiate between various species, and some are considered poisonous.

Pygmy Sperm Whales

Kogia breviceps

396 - 1005 Meters Deep

The pygmy sperm whale, a captivating addition to the impressive roster of marine creatures in the Philippines, is a relatively small whale that weighs only a few hundred pounds when fully grown. Divers can approach these whales without apprehension due to their manageable size.

Globehead Grenadier

Cetonurus Blobiceps

860 - 4621 Meters Deep

Both of its jaws consist of banded small pointed teeth. Body color ranges from brown to dark brown to camouflage the dark nature of the deep. Membranes all over the mouth, gill cavity, and body cavity are almost black in color. They mostly feed on small fishes and planktonic crustaceans. It has a gelatinous head that is bigger than its body, has a very short body or trunk, and a long tail that grows narrower to the end.

Shortspine Goosefish

Lophiodes Triradiatus

494 - 1560 Meters Deep

This species of goosefish is known for having 3 dorsal spines, wherein the second and the third spine is relatively short, and is furnished with tendrils.

Sloane's ViperFish

Chauliodus Sloani

500 - 2500 Meters Deep

This species is distinguished by its large mouth with long fang-like teeth that it can almost reach its eyes, and a long dorsal fin ray that stretches up to half of its body. This species goes in iridescent dark-blue color with pale fins, and the sides of its body is covered with hexagonal areas with each of it having one or small photophores that make it appear iridescent.

Bathypelagic Zone

This zone extends from 1,000 meters to 4,000 meters (3,000 to 13,000 ft) deep below the surface.

Temperature in this deep water is ice-cold, as its temperature does not go far beyond 4°C or 39°F.

Aside from its freezing water, the pressure in this zone reaches up to 5,850 pounds per square inch!

False Killer Whale

Pseudorca crassidens

4700 Meters Deep

False killer whales are named because the shape of their skulls, not their external appearance, is similar to that of killer whales. Reaching up to 6 meters in length, the species behaves much more like a smaller dolphin, swimming quickly, occasionally leaping, and sometimes approaching whale-watching vessels.

Tripod Fish

Bathypterois grallator

4800 Meters Deep

Their pelvic fins and caudal fin have long bony rays protruding from them. They face the current while standing still on their long rays. Once they sense food nearby, they use their large pectoral fins to hit the unsuspecting prey towards their mouth.

Sea Pig

Scotoplanes globosa

5500 Meters Deep

It is an echinoderm that looks like an ugly sea cucumber and can eat mud like an earthworm. Some echinoderms, like the swimming cucumber, use wing-like structures to swim as if they were flying through the water. They live only in the deepest, darkest, coldest parts of the ocean, as far as four miles under the water's surface.

Abyssopelagic Zone

This zone is a vast region of perpetual darkness stretching across 83% of the ocean floor and lies deep beneath the suns reach

It has a frigid temperature near freezing and crushing pressure. This extreme environment hosts unique creatures adapted to survive in its harshness.

Snailfish

Liparidae

8300 Meters Deep

The snailfish or liparidae are one of the species that are found in the Hadal zone, these creatures are small that grows to a maximum length of 12 inches. They are tadpole-shaped fishes with loose and scaleless skins, but sometimes they have prickly ones.

Decapoda Shrimp

Benthesicymus crenatus

8350 Meters Deep

Decapoda Shrimp are species that are found in various pacific trenches. This creature's body goes from reddish to orangish red color. Such color acts as their defense mechanism to the predators of the deep, as the red light does not reach this region thus their color appears to be black and is less visible to the wandering predators around.

Cusk-Eel

Abyssobrotula galatheae

8370 Meters Deep

Like the snailfish, cusk-eels are also a bottom-feeding marine fish family (Ophidiidae) with gooey skin. Despite the name, cusk-eels are actually more closely related to tuna than they are to true eels.

Hadalpelagic Zone

The ocean floor in most areas lies from 4000 to 6000 meters below the surface,

but when it comes to trenches it extends to up to 11,000 meters deep. Waters in these areas are above freezing point and of complete darkness, thus species commonly found in these waters have bioluminesce as their source of light.
